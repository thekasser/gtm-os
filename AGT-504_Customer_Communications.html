Customer Communications Agent
Executes all outbound transactional communications to customers: price increases, Terms of Service changes, and End of Sale / End of Life notices. Dual-track: generates an internal rep brief first (with priority flags for proactive outreach), then sends a formal external communication to the appropriate contacts after human approval. Never the detection layer — execution only.
L5 PricingConfig scan (price changes) CommEventQueue (EoS/EoL, ToS) Human approval required — never auto-sends New tables: CommEventQueue, CommBriefLog, CommDeliveryLog
Trigger model (locked): Hybrid. Price changes are auto-detected via PricingConfig diff scan — AGT-504 detects when a future-dated rate change is published and initiates the process automatically. EoS/EoL and ToS changes are strategic decisions made outside the GTM OS; they enter via manual entry to CommEventQueue (L0 table, RevOps-owned). AGT-504 is execution-only in both paths — it does not originate the decision to communicate, it executes the communication once the trigger condition is met or the queue entry is made.
Communication types — contact scope + source
Price Increase
Trigger
PricingConfig scan: future-dated rate change detected with effective_date set. Auto-initiates process; no manual queue entry required.
External contact
Billing contact on order form — written by L8 AGT-801, stored in OrderForm.billing_contact. Generic email detection logic applies (see below).
Pricing reference
PricingConfig (L0) — old rate, new rate, effective date, affected SKUs.
ToS Change
Trigger
Manual entry to CommEventQueue by Legal or RevOps. comm_type = 'tos_change'. Effective date and change summary required at entry.
External contact
Billing contact on order form + any contacts in Contacts table with persona = legal or signatory. Generic email detection logic applies.
Pricing reference
N/A. ToS comms reference the CommEventQueue change_summary field only.
EoS / EoL Notice
Trigger
Manual entry to CommEventQueue by Product. comm_type = 'eos_eol'. EoS and EoL treated identically — both require full user notification.
External contact
All active users in Contacts table for affected accounts (not billing contact only). If no Contacts records exist: fall back to billing contact + flag AM to identify users.
Affected accounts
Filtered by product/SKU match against active subscriptions. Only accounts with the affected product receive comm.
Trigger model detail
PricingConfig scan — price changes
AGT-504 scans PricingConfig daily for rows where effective_date is set in the future AND rate_delta is non-zero AND comm_initiated = FALSE. On detection: set comm_initiated = TRUE, create CommEventQueue record automatically, begin audience segmentation. No human needed to start the process — the published rate card IS the trigger.
CommEventQueue — EoS/EoL + ToS
RevOps or Product manually creates a CommEventQueue record. Required fields at entry: comm_type, affected_skus (nullable for ToS), effective_date, lead_time_days (default 3), change_summary, initiated_by. AGT-504 polls CommEventQueue for records where status = 'pending' and begins execution. RevOps owns the queue — no other team can initiate without RevOps entry or approval.
Internal brief — priority customer flagging
Priority customers require proactive AM outreach before the external comm goes out. Any account meeting one or more of the following conditions is flagged as priority in the internal brief. AM receives explicit call-to-action: contact this customer before the external comm sends.
Churn risk ≥ Medium
ChurnRiskLog.churn_risk_tier IN ('medium', 'high', 'critical'). A transactional comm hitting a at-risk account without AM preparation is a retention risk multiplier.
Source: ChurnRiskLog (AGT-502)
Renewal ≤ 90 days
Open renewal opportunity with close_date within 90 days. A price change or EoL comm landing during renewal negotiation must be AM-managed.
Source: Opportunities
ABM active
Accounts.abm_active = TRUE. Bespoke relationship accounts should never receive a transactional comm without prior AM context-setting.
Source: Accounts
ACV threshold
Configurable per comm event — set at CommEventQueue creation. Default: top 25% ACV within segment. RevOps can raise or lower per event.
Source: CommEventQueue.acv_priority_threshold
Lead time window: Default 3 business days between internal brief delivery and external comm send. Configurable per event via CommEventQueue.lead_time_days. Clock starts when internal brief is delivered to AMs — not when the comm event is created. Human approval of the external comm draft can happen in parallel during this window; send is gated by both approval AND window expiry.

Generic billing email detection path
Step 1 — Detection: Regex pattern match on billing_contact email. Patterns: billing@, invoices@, ap@, accounts-payable@, finance@, noreply@, and similar. If matched: set generic_email_flag = TRUE.
Step 2 — Human contact search: Query Contacts table for account — any record with is_human = TRUE. If found: add to recipient list alongside generic email (send to both).
Step 3 — Delay gate: If generic_email_flag = TRUE and no human contact found: delay external send by 1 business day. Flag AM immediately with specific ask: "Identify a human billing or financial contact at [Account] — required before price change communication sends."
Step 4 — Resolution: If AM identifies contact within 1 business day: add to recipient list, proceed. If 1 business day passes with no resolution: send to generic email anyway. Log unresolved_generic_flag = TRUE on CommDeliveryLog. AM is not blocking the comm — delay is a courtesy window only.
Step 5 — Enrichment trigger: Regardless of resolution, trigger AGT-204 enrichment request for this account to find billing/financial contacts. Enrichment result populates Contacts table for future comm events — this is the persistent fix, not the delay.
Execution flow
1
Detect or receive trigger
PricingConfig scan (daily): detect future-dated rate delta, auto-create CommEventQueue record. OR: CommEventQueue poll — status = 'pending' record found (EoS/EoL or ToS). Set status = 'in_progress'. All subsequent steps reference this CommEventQueue record as the event anchor.
2
Audience segmentation
Identify all affected accounts. For price changes: accounts with affected SKU in active subscription. For EoS/EoL: accounts with affected product. For ToS: all active customer accounts. Output: affected account list with ACV, churn tier, renewal date, ABM status.
3
Contact validation
For each affected account: resolve contacts per comm type logic above. Run generic email detection. Flag any accounts needing resolution. Set 1-day delay timer for unresolved generic email accounts.
4
Generate internal brief (per AM)
One brief per AM scoped to their book of affected accounts. Sections: (a) event summary — what is changing, effective date, pricing delta or product change; (b) your affected accounts — full list with ACV, current churn risk tier, renewal proximity; (c) priority customers — accounts meeting any priority flag, each with specific call-to-action and recommended talking points; (d) window — "External communication sends in [N] business days. Contact priority customers before then." Deliver immediately on brief generation — clock starts here.
5
Generate external comm draft
Draft transactional communication per comm type. Price increase: formal notice with old rate, new rate, effective date, SKU scope, and any transition terms from PricingConfig. ToS: change summary, effective date, acceptance instructions. EoS/EoL: product sunset timeline, migration path if available (from CommEventQueue.change_summary), support window. Draft written per recipient type — billing contact vs active user may receive different framing.
6
Human approval gate
External comm draft submitted to RevOps (all comm types) + Comms/Legal (ToS and EoS/EoL). Approver reviews: content accuracy, tone, contact list, timing. Approval can happen in parallel with the internal brief lead time window — they are independent gates. Both must clear before external send proceeds. Set CommEventQueue.approval_status = 'approved' + approval_by + approval_timestamp.
7
External comm send gate check
Two conditions must both be true before send: (a) approval_status = 'approved', (b) lead_time_days window has elapsed since internal brief delivery. If generic email delay is active on any accounts: those accounts wait their additional 1 business day. All other approved accounts send on schedule.
8
Send + log
Send external comm to resolved contact list. Write one CommDeliveryLog record per recipient per event: contact, delivery timestamp, comm type, generic_email_flag, unresolved_generic_flag. Update CommEventQueue.status = 'sent'. Write CommBriefLog completion record.
Dual-gate before external send
Both gates run in parallel. External comm does not send until both clear. The lead time window protects the AM relationship; the approval gate protects content and legal accuracy.

Key decisions: Hybrid trigger model (PricingConfig scan + CommEventQueue manual entry). Human approval always required — never auto-sends. Priority flags: churn ≥ Medium, renewal ≤90d, ABM active, ACV threshold (configurable, default segment P75). Generic email: 1-day delay + AM flag, then send regardless. Lead time: configurable, default 3 business days.
