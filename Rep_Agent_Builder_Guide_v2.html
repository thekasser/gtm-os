Rep Agent Builder Guide
Build personal workflow and briefing agents on top of GTM OS data and agent outputs.
How this works: The GTM OS runs continuously — scoring accounts, tracking deals, monitoring customers, measuring performance. All of that work is written to structured tables. Your personal agent reads from those tables to build exactly the briefing or workflow tool you want, without duplicating any of the underlying logic. This guide tells you which tables and agents to reference, and what data is available in each.
v2 — updated for L5–L7: This version adds customer health, expansion, churn risk, QBR prep, onboarding status, coaching plan, and performance tables now available from L5 (Post-Sale), L6 (Customer Lifecycle), and L7 (Measurement & Feedback) agents.

Guardrails — what to do and not do
doAlways filter by your rep ID
Most tables carry employee_crm_id, assigned_am, or assigned_csm. Always scope queries to your ID. Without a filter your agent will pull data across the whole org.
don'tRe-score or re-route anything
ICP scores, priority scores, health scores, and routing assignments are all managed by the GTM OS. Your agent surfaces these — it doesn't recalculate them. Use rep_pin in AccountPriorityScore if you want to flag a disagreement.
doReference tables by name
"Pull from CustomerHealthLog written by AGT-501" is clearer and more reliable than describing the data you want from scratch. The agent knows the table structure when you name it explicitly.
don'tDuplicate GTM OS logic
If you're writing prompts like "score my accounts based on…" or "decide which customers are at risk…" — stop. The GTM OS already did that work. Your agent presents those decisions, it doesn't re-make them.
doUse coaching plan data, not raw metrics
RepCoachingLog has your structured coaching recommendations already synthesized by AGT-701. Use that rather than trying to derive coaching insights from raw ConvIntelligence or OpportunityBriefLog scoring fields yourself.
don'tShare internal-only QBR content externally
QBRLog contains both an internal brief and a customer-facing artifact. The internal brief includes churn risk language and ACV estimates that are not for customer consumption. Always use the customer_artifact field for anything going to the customer.
