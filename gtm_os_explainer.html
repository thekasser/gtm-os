A complete GTM
Operating System

Every job RevOps does — quota planning, lead routing, deal health, churn risk, customer comms, business reviews — specced as a service. Three reasoning agents on top read those services to answer real CRO questions. 41 services + 3 agents + 15 specialist tools across 10 layers.

A quick tour for first-time viewers
The logic of how every job is done is no longer trapped in someone's head or a stale Confluence doc — it's a click-through service spec, with reasoning agents (the L9 "brains") that answer questions a CRO actually asks: why is the segment soft? what's the play on this account? did last year's bet work? Brains propose; humans approve; services execute. The full lineage from a brain proposal to an executed action is preserved end-to-end. Click the tabs above; here's what each one shows.
The OS — every service catalogued, click-through to the full logic spec for any one. Replaces "ask Sarah" or "find the Confluence doc" — the actual logic of how each job is done is no longer tribal knowledge.
Connection Map — the dependency graph between services. RevOps is a team sport: a new product ripples through quotas, enablement, comp, WBR. This is the visual of those connections — manual today, scales through the OS in the bigger vision.
Brain Outputs — 14 real questions a CRO might ask, each answered by the prototype's reasoning agents on real data. What it actually feels like to ask the system a question — not a slide deck claim.
Follow a Lead — one synthetic deal, walked end-to-end from inbound lead through renewal, with every service firing + two real brain reasoning moments embedded inline. Answers "does this thing actually work?" with the full lifecycle.
Planning — your quarterly planning meeting in three sub-panels: quota, headcount, territory. Pull a slider; the math respects every spec guardrail. Sized for $400M–$1B ARR / 80–100 reps.
Forecast & WBR — the forecast call (bottoms-up vs top-down vs commit vs plan) and the WBR readout (5 canonical metrics + brain narrative). The numbers your CFO and board see.
ICP Scorer Demo — deterministic JS reproduction of the 6-dimension ICP scoring + routing. Skip unless you want the dimensional model up close.

Three tiers. 41 GTM Services in L1–L8 (deterministic, single-writer-per-table, audit-grade — v38 added AGT-208 Developer Signal Scorer for PLG-funnel scoring on consumption-pricing platforms). 3 Brain Agents in L9 (LLM-native, operator-invoked, read Tier 1 services but never write canonical data; gated by 30-question eval harness — AGT-901 / AGT-902 prototyped & eval-passing, AGT-903 specced with build deferred). 15 Specialist Tools in Tier 3 across 4 waves — stateless callable functions that brains and services invoke for narrow cognition tasks (TOOL-003 / TOOL-004 / TOOL-008 / TOOL-010 prototyped; remaining 11 specced including TOOL-015 Consumption-Margin Decomposer added v38 for tier-migration GP attribution). The distinction is deliberate; brains propose, humans co-define and approve, services execute. v38 also adds tier-migration as a new expansion play type (AGT-503), GP-overlay comp variant (AGT-101 + AGT-103), and motion-aware lead routing (AGT-202). See changelog for current state.
