AGT-801 — Order Management

Layer 8: Revenue Operations · Accepted quote → order form → billing system handoff · Mid-term amendments · Finance-owned
L8 · Agent 01 Specced · v25 Schema adds: OrderLog, OrderLineItems, OrderApprovalLog, AmendmentLog
Purpose

AGT-801 is the first agent in L8 Revenue Operations. It converts an accepted AGT-406 quote into a legally binding order form, routes it through the appropriate approval chain, and hands it off to the billing system. It also owns mid-term contract amendments — seat expansions, SKU additions, and pricing corrections. AGT-801 re-scores the deal independently of AGT-406; the order form introduces contractual terms (payment terms, commit structure, legal entity) not present in the quote, so the score inputs are materially different.

Order form contents

The order form is a legal document — not a pricing proposal. It includes everything in the accepted quote plus the following contractual terms:

Billing contact on the order form (written by AGT-801) is the target for AGT-504 Customer Communications on future price change events. This is the integration point that makes AGT-504 contact routing work.
Deal scoring (re-run at order stage)

AGT-801 scores the order form independently of AGT-406's quote score. Order form inputs include contractual terms not in the quote — payment terms, commit frequency, auto-renewal structure, legal entity. Score drives approval routing.

Score bandCriteriaApproval required
Green (75–100)Pricing within standard bounds. Standard contractual terms. No policy exceptions.Auto-approve. No human approval required.
Yellow (50–74)Pricing discount above standard threshold OR non-standard contractual terms (but within policy).Yellow pricing → Finance Director. Yellow terms → Legal Director. Tracks are independent — both may fire simultaneously.
Red (0–49)Pricing significantly outside standard bounds OR materially non-standard terms (indemnification, liability caps, IP ownership deviations).Red pricing → Finance VP + CRO. Red terms → Legal VP + CRO. Both tracks must clear independently. CRO approves on pricing track regardless of legal outcome.
Two-track parallel approval: Legal approves its terms independently. Finance + CRO approve pricing and deal terms. Both tracks must clear before the order form is sent to the customer. If only one track is triggered (e.g. yellow pricing, green terms), only that track's approval is required.
Pricing corrections always require Finance Director minimum approval regardless of score — a correction implies a prior error, which is an audit event. Score-based routing applies on top of this floor.

Amendment typeWhat changesApproach

Amendments reference the original OrderLog record via AmendmentLog.order_id. The original order is not overwritten — it is versioned. AGT-802 sees the amendment and adjusts future billing accordingly.
Billing system handoff

On approval, AGT-801 writes the structured OrderLog + OrderLineItems payload and signals AGT-802 to begin billing cycle setup. The billing system (Stripe, Zuora, NetSuite — external) receives the structured payload. AGT-801 does not interact directly with the billing system API — it writes to the OS schema and the billing system integration layer reads from it. This keeps the OS integration-agnostic.
