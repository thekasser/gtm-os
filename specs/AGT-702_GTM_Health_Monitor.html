AGT-702 — GTM Health Monitor

Layer 7: Measurement & Feedback · Canonical GTM efficiency metrics · Cadenced snapshot model
L7 · Agent 02 Specced · v21 Schema adds: MetricsCalcLog, FP&APlan versioning extension
Purpose

AGT-702 is the canonical source for GTM efficiency metrics. It computes, persists, and reports the five core metrics — Magic Number, Rule of 40 (three variants), NRR, GRR, and CAC Payback — on a cadenced snapshot basis. It measures actuals against FP&APlan versioned targets and prior period benchmarks, and routes metric snapshots to AGT-704 for WBR/MBR synthesis. It does not produce narrative or calibration recommendations — those belong to AGT-704. AGT-702 is metrics computation and reporting only.

Canonical metrics

Five metric families. All computed values written to MetricsCalcLog as the canonical persisted source. Downstream agents read from MetricsCalcLog — never recompute.

1. Magic Number
Magic Number = Net New Revenue (period) ÷ S&M Spend (prior period)
Revenue basisRevenue (not ARR) — canonical, never changes Net new revenueNew logo revenue + expansion revenue − churn revenue for the period S&M spendPayoutHistory (TI spend) + MarketingHC loaded cost + sales HC loaded cost from CapacityPlan. Same basis used for CAC Payback. Source tablesRevenue, PayoutHistory, CapacityPlan, SpendByQuarter, MarketingHC Target>1.0 = efficient growth; <0.75 = efficiency concern CadenceMonthly snapshot (quarterly trailing average also persisted)
2. Rule of 40 — Three variants
Variant A: Revenue Growth % + EBITDA Margin %
Variant B: Revenue Growth % + FCF Margin %
Variant C: Revenue Growth % + Operating Margin %
Why threeEBITDA, FCF, and operating margin tell different stories about cost discipline and capital efficiency. All three stored independently in MetricsCalcLog — not aggregated. Revenue growthYoY revenue growth % from Revenue table Margin sourcesFinance-provided via SpendByQuarter. If FCF or EBITDA not available, variant flagged as data_gap in MetricsCalcLog — does not block other variants. Target≥40 combined = healthy; all three variants tracked independently CadenceQuarterly (monthly revenue growth available; margin data quarterly)
3. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
NRR = (Beginning ARR + Expansion − Churn − Downsell) ÷ Beginning ARR × 100
Source tablesRevenue, ChurnRiskLog (AGT-502 churn actuals), ExpansionLog (AGT-503 expansion actuals) Target>110% — canonical threshold, never changes Weekly inputAGT-502 NRR risk summary feeds forward-looking NRR trajectory. Actuals reconciled monthly. CadenceMonthly actuals + weekly trajectory from AGT-502 risk summary
4. Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)
GRR = (Beginning ARR − Churn − Downsell) ÷ Beginning ARR × 100
Distinction from NRRGRR excludes expansion — measures pure retention quality. NRR can mask churn with expansion. Both required. Source tablesRevenue, ChurnRiskLog (AGT-502 actuals) Target>85% — canonical threshold, never changes CadenceMonthly
5. CAC Payback Period
CAC Payback = CAC ÷ (ACV × Gross Margin %) expressed in months
CACTotal S&M spend ÷ new logos acquired in period. S&M spend same basis as Magic Number. Gross marginFrom SpendByQuarter + Revenue — Finance-provided or computed from available cost data Source tablesRevenue, PayoutHistory, CapacityPlan, SpendByQuarter, Opportunities (new logo count) Target<18 months — canonical threshold, never changes CadenceQuarterly (monthly new logo counts too noisy for monthly CAC)
Plan vs actual comparison model

Every MetricsCalcLog row includes a plan comparison. AGT-702 measures actuals against four reference points simultaneously.

Reference pointSourceNotes
FP&APlan versioned targetFP&APlan — current plan version0+12 at year start. Rolls to 1+11, 2+10, etc. as actuals close. AGT-702 always reads the most current version via plan_version field.
Board-approved planFP&APlan — is_board_plan = TRUEMay differ from current operating plan. Stored as a separate version in FP&APlan. CRO and Finance use board plan as external benchmark.
Prior period actualsMetricsCalcLog — prior rowsQoQ and YoY comparison computed from persisted MetricsCalcLog history. This is why MetricsCalcLog must persist — prior period comparisons require historical rows.
AGT-402 forward forecastOpportunities, AGT-402 outputForward-looking: where is the business tracking vs plan for the remainder of the period? Lagging metrics compared to plan; leading forecast compared to plan target.
FP&APlan schema extension (v21): Three fields added — plan_version (string: "0+12", "1+11", "2+10", etc.), reforecast_as_of_date (date), is_board_plan (boolean). AGT-702, AGT-402, and AGT-404 always join on the most current plan_version for operating comparisons; is_board_plan flag isolates the external benchmark version.
Snapshot cadence
CadenceMetrics computedConsumer
WeeklyNRR trajectory (from AGT-502 weekly digest), pipeline coverage vs plan, AGT-402 forward forecast vs FP&APlanAGT-704 WBR, RevOps
MonthlyMagic Number, NRR actuals, GRR, all R40 variants (if margin data available), all plan comparisonsAGT-704 MBR, RevOps, CRO (via AGT-704)
QuarterlyCAC Payback, R40 variants (margin finalized), trailing 4Q averages for all metricsAGT-704 exec QBR, board package
No continuous monitoring. Snapshot cadence is correct for efficiency metrics. Mid-period deterioration on lagging metrics (Magic Number, R40, CAC Payback) reflects planned investment activity, not operational failure. Fast-moving alerts live in AGT-401, AGT-502, and AGT-402 — the agents closest to those signals.
Alert routing

AGT-702 routes to RevOps only. RevOps triages and escalates. AGT-702 never routes directly to CRO, Finance, or individual reps.

ConditionAlert typeRecipient
Magic Number < 0.75 for two consecutive periodsEfficiency flagRevOps → RevOps triages CRO escalation
NRR trajectory below 110% thresholdRetention risk flagRevOps (AGT-502 also fires directly to AM — not duplicated here)
GRR below 85%Churn quality flagRevOps
CAC Payback > 18 monthsEfficiency flagRevOps
Any metric > 20% below FP&APlan target for current periodPlan miss flagRevOps → RevOps triages CRO + Finance escalation
Upstream data stale (MetricsCalcLog not refreshed on schedule)Data quality flagRevOps
RevOps is the single triage point for all AGT-702 alerts. This prevents alert fatigue at the CRO level and ensures context is added before escalation. If a sustained metric miss implies a need to revisit quota (AGT-101), capacity (AGT-105), or ICP weights (AGT-201), that recommendation surfaces through AGT-704 MBR synthesis. AGT-702 flags the miss; AGT-704 contextualizes it; humans decide.
