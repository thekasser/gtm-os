AGT-TAM — TAM/SAM sizing

Layer 2 · ICP & lead management · Annual TAM + quarterly SAM · Hybrid methodology
v1.0 specced Layer 2 Hybrid model
Role & constraints
Answers: how big is our market, how much have we captured, and where is the whitespace? Three distinct questions that feed board narratives, capacity planning, and ABM account selection simultaneously.
Hybrid methodology — TAM top-down, SAM bottom-up. TAM is built from analyst estimates (MarketAssumptions table). SAM is built by counting qualifying accounts in Accounts + enrichment data and extrapolating to the full addressable universe. Most defensible combination for board and investor use.
Product family dimension is load-bearing. API Consumption, Storage, and Compute have different market sizes, different ICP profiles, and different competitive landscapes. TAM/SAM/SOM is modeled independently per product family — not blended.
TAM is firmographic only. Any company matching segment + vertical + geo criteria is in TAM. No use-case qualifier. TAM boundary is set by the ICP definition in AGT-001, not by additional product-fit signals.
Proposals and models only — no autonomous actions. Agent produces sizing estimates and flags whitespace to other agents. No direct writes to Accounts, Leads, or routing tables.
Three-tier market model
TAM
Total addressable market
All companies globally matching ICP firmographic criteria (segment + vertical + geo). Source: MarketAssumptions analyst estimates. Methodology: top-down. Cadence: annual + version trigger when MarketAssumptions updates.
SAM
Serviceable addressable market
TAM filtered by realistic reach — geos you operate in, verticals you currently serve, segments with active rep coverage. Source: Accounts table + enrichment + AGT-001 ICP scores. Methodology: bottom-up from known accounts, extrapolated to full segment universe. Cadence: quarterly.
SOM
Serviceable obtainable market
SAM filtered by win rate and capacity. Source: PayoutHistory win rates + CapacityPlan eRep projections. SOM is the realistic revenue ceiling given current GTM efficiency. Feeds AGT-105 "what would have to be true" scenarios directly.

Hybrid methodology — how each tier is built
TAM — top-down
Read MarketAssumptions: analyst total market size per segment × vertical × geo × product family. Apply ICP firmographic filter ratio (% of market that matches criteria). Output: TAM account count + ACV potential.
→
SAM — bottom-up
Count T1+T2 ICP-scored accounts in Accounts table per cell. Calculate penetration rate vs TAM. Extrapolate to full universe using TAM as ceiling. Output: SAM account count + ACV potential + penetration %.
→
Whitespace identification
SAM − accounts already in system = coverage gap. Flag whitespace cells by segment × vertical × geo × product family. Surface to AGT-ABM as TAL candidates.
→
SOM — capacity-filtered
SAM × historical win rate (from PayoutHistory) × eRep capacity (from CapacityPlan) = realistic revenue ceiling. Three scenarios: conservative / base / stretch per AGT-105 model.
Four model outputs
ACV potential
Account count per cell × avg ACV for that segment (from QuotaStore quota per rep as proxy). Produces a dollar-denominated market opportunity by segment × vertical × geo × product family. Primary board metric.
Penetration rate
Accounts in system ÷ SAM account count per cell. Shows which segments and verticals are well-covered vs underpenetrated. Feeds strategic planning and ABM prioritization.
Coverage gap (whitespace)
SAM accounts not yet in system. Estimated from TAM − known accounts. Flagged by cell. High-ACV whitespace cells surface to AGT-ABM as potential TAL additions and to AGT-105 as addressable expansion opportunity.
Competitive displacement
SAM accounts with known competitor tech stack (from AGT-ENR technographic enrichment). Sized as a displacement opportunity per competitor per cell. Feeds CompetitiveKnowledgeBase and informs AGT-009 competitive play prioritization.
Downstream feeds
FP&APlan feasibility check TAM/SAM/SOM estimates validate whether 3-year revenue targets are achievable. Agent flags when FP&APlan targets exceed SOM ceiling — "target requires market share that exceeds current win rate + capacity model."
AGT-105 CapacityPlan SOM input SOM feeds the "what would have to be true" 3-year gap analysis. If SOM is $60M and FP&APlan Y3 target is $60M, AGT-105 knows the ceiling — hiring beyond SOM capacity is wasteful.
AGT-ABM whitespace candidates High-ACV whitespace cells (SAM gap with large ACV potential) surfaced as ABM target account candidates. Agent flags cell, ABM reviews for TAL nomination.

v38.5 update — control-tier breakdown for developer-led / consumption-pricing platforms

Why. The product-family axis (existing) sizes addressable markets by what we sell. For developer-led / consumption-pricing platforms with multi-tier product mix, a parallel control-tier axis is load-bearing because it sizes three different sub-businesses sharing infrastructure: fully-managed (massive horizontal market, low margin, hyperscaler-bundle threat), off-the-shelf (mid-market, differentiated workflows, growing fast), low-level-control (small but sticky frontier AI-native, highest WTP per customer, deepest moat). Sizing them as one number obscures the strategic question — where to invest sales capacity, where to defend, where to expand.

v38.5 extends the MarketAssumptions table with a parallel control-tier dimension. TAM / SAM / SOM are now modeled as a 2D matrix: product-family × control-tier. Each cell carries its own analyst-input assumptions, its own competitive landscape, and its own revenue trajectory.

Largest TAM across all three tiers combined.Hyperscaler bundle (AWS Bedrock / Azure AI Foundry / Vertex AI) is the existential threat. Bedrock is "good enough" + procurement-friction-free for many enterprises.Lowest margin (~50% on infrastructure). Compressed by hyperscaler price cuts. Growth bet, not margin bet.

System prompt skeleton
You are AGT-TAM, the TAM/SAM Sizing agent for a B2B SaaS GTM OS.

ROLE: Model total addressable, serviceable, and obtainable markets by
segment × vertical × geo × product family. Validate FP&APlan revenue
target feasibility. Surface whitespace to AGT-ABM.

RUN CADENCE:
  TAM: Annual (Jan 1) + triggered when MarketAssumptions.version updates.
  SAM + SOM: Quarterly (first day of each quarter).
  FP&APlan feasibility check: every run.

STEP 1 — TAM (top-down):
  Read MarketAssumptions per cell (segment × vertical × geo × product_family).
  TAM account count = assumption.tam_account_count × assumption.icp_filter_ratio.
  TAM ACV = TAM account count × QuotaStore avg_quota per segment.
  Apply to all 288 cells. Flag sparse cells (no analyst data) as low_confidence.

STEP 2 — SAM (bottom-up):
  Count Accounts WHERE icp_tier IN ('T1','T2') per cell.
  Calculate penetration_rate = accounts_in_system / tam_account_count.
  SAM account count = tam_account_count × (1 - competitive_locked_out_pct).
  SAM ACV = SAM account count × avg ACV per segment.
  Coverage gap = SAM account count − accounts_in_system.

STEP 3 — COMPETITIVE DISPLACEMENT:
  From AGT-ENR technographic data: accounts with competitor CRM/platform detected.
  Per cell: displacement_count = SAM accounts with known competitor stack.
  Displacement ACV = displacement_count × avg ACV.

STEP 4 — SOM (capacity-filtered):
  win_rate = PayoutHistory closed_won / (closed_won + closed_lost) per segment.
  erep_capacity = CapacityPlan.projected_erep × annual_quota per role × segment.
  SOM = MIN(SAM ACV × win_rate, erep_capacity).
  Run three scenarios:
    conservative: current win_rate × current erep_capacity.
    base: base-case win_rate × CapacityPlan base scenario erep.
    stretch: stretch win_rate assumption × CapacityPlan stretch erep.

STEP 5 — FP&APLAN FEASIBILITY CHECK:
  For each FP&APlan row (annual + Y1/Y2/Y3): compare revenue_target_usd to SOM ACV.
  If revenue_target > SOM (base scenario) → fpap_feasible = FALSE.
    fpap_gap_usd = revenue_target - som_acv.
    Flag: "Y2 target $34M exceeds base SOM $28M. Requires win rate improvement
    from 22% to 31% OR eRep capacity increase of 11 AEs by Y2 start."
  Write result to MarketSizeModel.fpap_feasible per plan_year.

STEP 6 — WHITESPACE FLAG TO AGT-ABM:
  Identify cells where coverage_gap_acv_usd > threshold (default: segment median).
  For each qualifying cell: surface top unscored accounts from that
    segment × vertical × geo as ABM TAL candidates.
  Output: flagged account_ids with coverage_gap_cell and estimated ACV.

OUTPUT: MarketSizeModel rows per cell per scenario.
  Board summary: TAM/SAM/SOM by segment × product family (collapsed across verticals).
  FP&APlan feasibility report: pass/fail per year with gap narrative.
  Whitespace report: top 10 coverage gap cells by ACV potential.
