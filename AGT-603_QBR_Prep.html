AGT-603 · Layer 6 · Customer Lifecycle
QBR Prep Agent
Generates quarterly business review materials for every active customer account. Dual output: an internal prep brief (split by AM commercial role and CSM operational role) and a structured customer-facing summary artifact the CSM edits before presenting. Synthesizes health, usage, expansion signals, and MAP value event progress into a single coherent QBR narrative.
New agent New table: QBRLog Reads: AGT-501 CustomerHealthLog, AGT-503 ExpansionLog, MutualActionPlan, Opportunities
Trigger model
Quarterly auto-trigger
Default cadence: QBR prep fires X days before the quarterly QBR due date (configurable, default 10 business days). QBR due date set per account at onboarding close or manually by CSM. If no QBR date is set, agent prompts CSM to schedule. Quarterly cycle auto-advances after each completed QBR.
Meeting booking trigger
If a QBR meeting is booked on calendar before the auto-trigger fires: booking event triggers prep immediately (whichever comes first). Prevents the case where a CSM schedules a QBR for next week and prep hasn't started yet. Meeting booking signal sourced from RepActivity calendar entries.

Dual output model
Internal prep brief
Full 7-section brief with internal context, risk flags, AM/CSM role assignments, and talking points. Includes sections the customer never sees (e.g., churn risk tier language, expansion ACV potential, renewal approach recommendation). Delivered to AM + CSM separately — each receives their role-scoped sections highlighted. Delivered 10 business days before QBR (or immediately on meeting booking).
Customer-facing summary artifact
Structured summary document (sections 1, 2, 3, 6, 7 — usage, ROI, health narrative, open issues, next commitments). Expansion and renewal sections are internal-only — they inform AM/CSM talking points but are not in the customer document. CSM edits before sending or presenting. Agent generates the first draft; human always reviews before customer sees it.
